Props rendered in different manga techniques. Reference art_prop.md for the base prop templates.

### Technique Variants

**Pure Ink (Sumi-e Influence)**
- Bold single-stroke outlines with intentional brush texture
- No screentone — all shading via ink wash gradients and cross-hatching
- Negative space used aggressively for highlights
- Dry-brush effect on weathered surfaces
- Minimalist approach: convey form with fewest possible strokes

**Heavy Screentone**
- Multiple screentone densities layered for depth (10%, 30%, 50%, 70%)
- Dot-pattern transitions at curved surfaces
- Sand-gradient screentone for rough textures (stone, bark, rust)
- Line-screen tone for metallic surfaces
- Gradient screentone for atmospheric perspective on distant props

**Action-Focused Rendering**
- Speed lines integrated into the prop itself (blade trails, energy wakes)
- Motion blur streaks extending from prop edges
- Impact frame style: prop frozen at moment of contact with radial action lines
- Afterimage rendering: semi-transparent ghost copies trailing the prop
- Debris particles breaking off from the prop at impact point

**Close-Up Detail (Genga-Quality)**
- Extreme close-up of key prop details (engraving on blade, texture on scroll seal)
- Line-art refined to key-frame animation quality
- Cross-section cutaway showing internal mechanism
- Material callouts: labeled arrows in clean technical-font pointing to material types
- Scale reference: human hand silhouette next to prop for size comparison

**Color-Accented Manga**
- Primary monochrome ink rendering
- Single accent color in specific areas: crimson on blood/energy, electric cyan on magical glow
- Color applied as flat cel-shaded layer over the ink base
- Screentone still present in non-accented areas
- Color saturation deliberately restrained — not full-color, just strategic emphasis

**Atmospheric / Environmental**
- Prop partially obscured by mist/smoke rendered with soft screentone fade
- Rain-streaked prop with vertical line overlays
- Prop casting dramatic shadow on ground plane (low-angle light)
- Prop glowing in darkness: rim-light on edges, screentone for ambient light falloff
