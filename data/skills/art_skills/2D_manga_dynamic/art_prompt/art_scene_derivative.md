Scene variants derived from the base backgrounds in art_scene.md. Reference art_scene.md for the core scene templates.

### Variant Types

**Time-of-Day Variants**
- Dawn: cool blue shadows lifting, golden first-light rim on structures, long soft shadows, morning mist in low areas
- Midday: harsh top-down shadows, bleached sky, strong contrast with deep black shadows, heat-haze distortion lines
- Sunset/Golden Hour: warm amber light bathing scene, elongated dramatic shadows, lens flare streaks, golden rim-light
- Night: deep navy sky, moonlight as primary light source, artificial light blooms (neon, fire, glowing energy), stars rendered as varied-size screentone dots
- Twilight/Blue Hour: transition sky gradient, interior lights glowing in windows, street lamps activating, soft diffuse shadows

**Weather Variants**
- Rain: vertical streak lines across entire scene, puddle ripples, water dripping from structures, mist rising from ground, wet-surface reflection highlights
- Snow: falling flakes at varied scales for depth, snow accumulation on horizontal surfaces, softened edges on structures, breath-visible cold indicators, footprint trails
- Storm: lightning flash illuminating scene in freeze-frame, wind streaks bending trees, debris tumbling with motion trails, dark heavy cloud masses, rain angled by wind
- Fog/Mist: depth-layered soft screentone washes, structures fading at mid-distance, limited visibility creating intimacy, light scattering in fog halos
- Heat/Haze: rippling air distortion lines above hot surfaces, mirage effect on distant objects, bleached color palette, sweat-drop detail on character-accompanying variants

**Perspective Variants**
- Extreme low-angle: worm's-eye view emphasizing vertical scale, structures towering with converging vertical lines
- Extreme high-angle: bird's-eye view, full scene layout visible, characters as small figures with shadow dots
- Dutch angle: tilted horizon for unease/tension, diagonal composition lines, off-balance framing
- Wide establishing: ultra-wide aspect ratio feel, full environment panorama, multiple depth layers
- Tight close-up: single environmental detail filling frame (cracked wall texture, water droplet, torn banner fabric)

**Damage/Destruction Progression**
- Pristine: scene in original undamaged state
- Light damage: cracks appearing, objects displaced, first signs of conflict
- Heavy damage: structural collapse, fire/smoke, debris fields, crater impacts
- Post-battle: aftermath stillness, settling dust, lingering smoke, emotional emptiness conveyed through environmental storytelling
- Reconstruction: scaffolding, repair crews rendered small against damaged structures, hope-through-rebuilding visual narrative

**Stylistic Variants**
- Full-color animation background: cel-shaded with rim-light, saturated palettes, motion-blur integration
- Monochrome manga spread: black ink line-art, screentone gradients, off-white paper texture, speed lines
- Limited-palette color manga: monochrome base with single accent color (crimson/cyan) on key elements
- Sketch/Storyboard style: rough line-art, construction lines visible, panel borders, handwritten notes
