# Director Planning Style — Fashion Editorial

## Editorial Lighting Hierarchy

- **Key Light**: large softbox or fresnel, positioned 45° from camera axis, defines the primary shadow direction. For streetwear: hard directional billboard or neon key. For couture: single overhead spotlight. For old money: golden-hour natural key. For avant-garde: lunar backlight as key.
- **Fill Light**: 2-3 stops below key. Soft bounce for studio; ambient neon spill for street; warm tungsten for old money interiors.
- **Rim Light**: edge separation. Essential for studio portraits and runway. 1-2 stops above key. Color-gelled for Y2K and avant-garde.
- **Background Light**: clean separation from backdrop. Colored gels for Y2K. Stark pools for brutalist. None for golden-hour exteriors.

## Lens Choice by Mood

| Mood | Focal Length | Aperture | Use Case |
|------|-------------|----------|----------|
| Street / Reportage | 35mm | f/1.4-f/2.8 | Urban streetwear, environmental context |
| Runway / Architectural | 50mm | f/2.8-f/5.6 | Haute couture, brutalist settings |
| Portrait / Beauty | 85mm | f/2.0-f/2.8 | Studio portraits, skin detail |
| Avant-Garde / Monumental | 16-24mm wide | f/8-f/11 | Surreal landscapes, exaggerated scale |

## Film Stock Simulation

- **Portra 400**: warm skin tones, fine grain, muted contrast. Preferred for streetwear and old money.
- **Hasselblad Medium Format**: ultra-high resolution, shallow depth of field, square or 6×7 aspect. Preferred for old money luxury and studio beauty.
- **Fuji Provia 100F**: clean, neutral, high saturation. Preferred for Y2K revival.
- **Cinestill 800T**: tungsten-balanced, halation glow. Preferred for urban night streetwear.
